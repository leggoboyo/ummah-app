Mission

What the project is trying to do

Keep the essentials light

The app is structured so the core worship utilities do not depend on a live backend. The product goal is low monetary cost, low performance cost, and low hidden behavior.

Make sourcing visible

Quran, hadith, fiqh, and scholar-feed surfaces are expected to show where the content comes from, what version is installed, and what the original provider allows.

Keep optional features visibly optional

Billing, remote hadith pack delivery, scholar feed refresh, and BYOK AI are optional paths. They should not quietly become the free-core launch path.

Offline boundary

What stays on-device and what reaches out

Always on-device

  • Prayer calculations
  • Adhan scheduling after permissions are granted
  • Qibla bearing math
  • Hijri date logic
  • Bundled Quran Arabic reading

Offline after user download

  • Quran translations cached locally
  • Installed Sunni Hadith packs
  • Fiqh starter/reference content

Optional online only

  • RevenueCat and store billing flows
  • Cloudflare-delivered hadith packs
  • Scholar feed refresh
  • OpenAI BYOK assistant requests

Architecture

How the product is structured

01

Flutter app shell

The app shell lives in mobile/app and composes the feature packages. The launch path privileges the free core and defers optional modules.

02

Feature packages

Prayer, qibla, Quran, hadith, fiqh, subscriptions, scholar feed, and AI assistant are separated into packages so optional features do not own the free-core runtime.

03

Optional Cloudflare edge

Remote hadith pack manifests, access grants, and downloads are served from a Cloudflare Worker backed by KV and R2. That boundary has been verified and remains optional rather than mandatory.
